PREVENT INVASIVE ALIEN SPECIES ON LAND AND
IN WATER ECOSYSTEMS


By 2020, introduce measures to prevent the introduction and significantly reduce the impact of invasive alien species on land and water ecosystems and control or eradicate the priority species.

Protection is the most cost-effective and risk-free method of dealing with invasive species. Detecting invasive species early and responding quickly is far more effective than trying to control a widespread pest problem.

If complete eradication is not possible, the invasive species may be subject to control and management efforts. There are several methods of controlling invasive species:

Biological control is the deliberate manipulation of real enemies by humans for the purpose of controlling pests and reducing the population of invasive species by using prey that targets the invasive species. Controlling invasive populations with animals, fungi, or illnesses native to the targeted species' home range.

Pesticides, weedkillers, fungicides, and insecticides are all examples of chemical control.

Cultural control entails manipulating habits to increase invasives' mortality or reduce their rate of harm (selection of pest-resistant crops, winter cover crops, changing planting dates). In addition, measures designed to change human behavior to address the issue of invasive species spread are included, such as using opportunities to educate people about procedures to raise awareness about invasive species prevention (signage, public awareness campaigns).

Mowing, hoeing, tilling, girdling, chopping, and establishing barriers with tools or machines are examples of mechanical control techniques.
